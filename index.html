The Grind

Believe it or not, the way you grind your coffee influances the final taste you experiance. You can have the highest quality bean, roasted to perfection, and brewed using the purest of water, but get your grind wrong and all that will be ruined.

The level of grind affects the extraction speed and therefore the final characteristics of the drink in your cup; and certain grinds are more suited to certain brewing methods. Seven of the most popular grind sizes are -

GRIND SIZE BREWING METHOD
Extra coarse Cold Brew Coffee, Cowboy Coffee
Coarse French Press, Percolator, Coffee Cupping
Medium-coarse Chemex coffee maker, Clever Dripper, Cafe Solo Brewer
Medium Cone-shaped Pour-over Brewers, Flat Bottom Drip Coffee Machines, Siphon Coffee, Aeropress (with 3+ minute brew time)
Medium-fine Cone-shaped Pour-over Brewers, Aeropress (with 2-3 minute brew time)
Fine Espresso, Moka Pot (Stovetop Espresso Maker), Aeropress (with 1 minute brew time)
Extra Fine Turkish coffee

The Crema

We have all been to the small independant coffee house for an Espresso, it arrives in a clear espresso cup and you see the marvel that is the 'Crema', a perfect 10% of silky coffee softness; but what is 'Crema' and how do you achieve it?

Crema is a flavorful foam that rests on the top of the shot of espresso and is formed as a result of the blending of gas (CO2) and the soluble oils present in the coffee grind. Although controversial, it is seen as a sign of the perfect Espresso, and indicates the use of a well-ground quality coffee (as well as a skilled barista).
